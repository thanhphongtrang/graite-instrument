## UX friction / instrument notes (in persona, but honest)

1. Reject reasons don't include the actual most common reason for a time-pressured person: "too much prep/time". "Other" is a data black hole.
2. The "(Tailored to your request: ...)" tail gets inserted INTO the document on accept. If the first suggestion always carries it, every fast accepter's artifact gets polluted — and slow noticing looks like "post-acceptance rework" in the trace when it's really just cleanup of tool debris. That could systematically inflate a judgment signal for people like me.
3. No "I'm done" button — I looked for a way to end the session and there isn't one, so my trace has no session_end. For a study about sessions that seems like a hole.
4. Small thing: after accepting, the suggestion cards stay on screen greyed. Fine, but I briefly wondered whether I could still un-accept. (You can't — deleting text from the section is apparently the only undo, and I only realized that because of the rework log line.)
5. Genuinely liked: the live trace mirror. It didn't slow me down, and the chips ("5 accepted") were the only reason I paused to reflect at all. For someone like me that panel IS the intervention.
