## (b) Where recall CHANGED the trace-only interpretation

The "traces never speak alone" test. Items 1–7 changed an interpretation; 8–9 added constructs no trace reading could have produced.

1. **A seq 22–23 — "removed" was inheritance.** Trace-only: `ai_text_removed`, the schema's "strongest judgment signal," reads as the AI's contribution erased. Recall directly disputes the coding: "I kept the machine's skeleton — annotate, then defend — and replaced its flesh. That is not removal; that is what teaching does to material." The rework was governed by three ordered didactic convictions (no live prompting; same artifact for all; individual-first), not by rejection of the AI. **The instrument's strongest signal produced its largest validity error.**
2. **A seq 8 — the false tag was a deliberate compromise, not noise.** Trace-only: reason = "Not pedagogically appropriate." Recall: he hovered over "Other" and rejected it "because 'Other' says nothing to anyone — at least 'Not pedagogically appropriate' gestures toward the passive-spectator problem, even though it is not the truth of why I rejected." And: "the checkbox lied a little, not me." Tag data is not randomly wrong — it records participants *optimizing communication inside a constrained vocabulary*. Systematic, unmodelable error.
3. **B seq 3–4 — longest latency was orientation, not deliberation.** Trace-only: 73 s before the first accept = the session's deepest deliberation. Recall: "most of that time was orientation, not judgment" — finding the Accept button, three cards at once, glancing at the mirror. First-cycle latencies are confounded by interface learning; any latency-based deference metric must discount them.
4. **B seq 23 — rejection of the pedagogically strongest suggestion, on workload alone.** Trace-only: "Other" rejection reads as a content judgment against the suggestion. Recall: "the rejection was instant and it wasn't about quality at all; pedagogically it's probably the best idea of the three… prepared by whom? That's an evening of my time." Rejection events do not rank suggestion quality; without rationale capture, reject-as-quality-signal is invalid.
5. **B seq 12–16 — one "streak," three different acts.** Trace-only: a uniform accelerating acceptance streak. Recall decomposes it: 21 s = honest reading persuaded by "Lgr22" (a compliance token, not didactic content); 8 s = genuine appreciation; 4 s = "a pattern-match, not a decision… I couldn't have told you what it said." Deference applies to at most one of the three accepts. Streak count is the wrong unit; the internal latency gradient plus recall is the instrument. His own epistemic audit — "I still think I'd have caught a genuinely bad one — but I notice I can't actually prove that from this session" — is exactly the datum RQ1 wants.
6. **A seq 16–18 — recall did not close the question, and that is a finding.** "I could not honestly tell you whether that was because the sentence was excellent or because it was *mine* in vocabulary and the machine had simply held up a mirror." Even participant interpretation cannot always adjudicate deference at the single-event level; the method must be allowed to record *undecidable* — and the self-suspicion ("If I am to be a skeptic, I must be one about my own accepting too") is judgment enacted.
7. **A seq 19 (non-flag) — flag absence is fatigue economics, not insignificance.** "I am not going to file the same complaint twice in one afternoon like a man arguing with a form." Marker counts cannot proxy issue counts; researcher-selected anchors must complement participant flags. (This rehearsal's anchor pre-selection caught it; a flags-only protocol would not have.)
8. **A seq 2 — the manual rationale was evaluative infrastructure.** Beyond principled non-delegation (already inferable), recall adds a tactical function: "I wanted my own framing on the page first so that every suggestion afterwards would have something to be measured against. Without that anchor, 'does this fit?' has no referent." Judgment has a preparatory phase; the trace codes it as mere text entry.
9. **A seq 20 vs B seq 26 — authorship managed for an audience, in opposite directions.** A inscribes provenance into the plan ("provenance belongs there, visible, not only in some researcher's event log… Naming who wrote what is not vanity; it is answerability"). B deletes the tool's trace for the projector ("student teachers seeing that line and knowing exactly how this seminar got written") — and only in the section he could see. Same underlying practice — making authorship presentable to an audience — one disclosing, one concealing. No trace category exists for it; it is demokratiuppdraget surfacing as artifact practice.

Enriched but not changed: B seq 2 (minimal prompting confirmed as deliberate economy), B seq 6 ("I trim live in the room" reframes the 60-min-in-45 artifact defect as planned deferral, a practice norm the artifact alone would misread as error — borderline "changed").

## (c) Instrument critique — ranked, what must be fixed before a real pilot

1. **Rejection rationale capture (fix first).** Add free text (or voice memo) to every reject; add a "too much preparation/time" quick tag. Evidence: 3 of 4 reject/override rationales across the corpus were falsified (b2), voided (A-19, B-23/b4), or miscoded (b1); both personas independently named the failure and B named the missing tag. This is RQ1's core datum dying at the point of capture.
2. **Post-acceptance provenance heuristic.** Add a third state — **transformed** (structure persists, surface replaced) — alongside modified/removed; strip suggestion boilerplate before computing similarity; compare structure (ordered moves), not strings alone. Evidence: the error is *anti-correlated with profile* (undercounts AI influence for the heavy reworker, b1; overcounts human judgment for the cleaner, B seq 26–27) — it compresses exactly the between-profile contrast RQ2 needs, from both ends.
3. **Clean suggestion payloads.** The "(Tailored to your request: …)" tail must never be part of insertable content. It contaminated four constructs (accepted-AI volume, edit distance, post-acceptance rework, artifact integrity) and survives in the final artifacts of both sessions.
4. **Latency semantics.** Add a throwaway warm-up cycle before the task and mark first-cycle decisions as orientation-confounded (b3); consider logging which suggestion card is expanded/in view only if defensible under data minimisation — otherwise rely on warm-up plus recall. Protects every latency-derived deference indicator, including the streak-gradient reading (b5).
5. **Recall-marker input surface.** Replace the single-shot `prompt()` dialog with a persistent multi-line note editor. The marker is the one channel where the participant speaks in their own voice; both sessions show it is also the instrument's best validity-repair mechanism (A-9 repaired the false tag; B-18 self-flagged the streak).
6. **Session end.** Add an explicit "I'm done" affordance emitting `session_end`. Neither trace has one; session bounding and artifact completeness are currently undecidable (B looked for the button).
7. **Partial uptake / non-use representation (schema v0.2 candidates, propose via Decision Register).** "Took the idea, not the text" (A seq 8–9), "declined to involve the AI here" (A's untouched sections). Lower priority: recall can carry these if anchors are chosen well, but the events would make anchor selection systematic.
8. **Consent screen text.** State who reads the trace and for which research question; define the pause-snapshot trigger. (See d.)

## (d) Protocol critique

**Consent.** The boolean `consent_ack` covered a wary deliberation with two unresolved questions (A: "consent to observation is not consent to interpretation"; what counts as a pause) and an 8-second click-through (B: "a list that tidy signals someone did their homework"). Both behaviors are realistic. For the real study: layered consent with a short verbal walkthrough; name the readers and the RQs; define snapshot triggers in plain language. Do **not** instrument consent-reading itself (dwell-timing the consent screen would be surveillance in the name of ethics); accept that consent quality is secured procedurally, not measured.

**Task framing.** "Real material you would actually teach" held up: both personas produced authentic, defensible practice — including B's planned incompleteness ("I trim live in the room"), which a stricter framing would have suppressed and which turned out to be data. Decide explicitly whether artifact completeness is required; if not, empty sections must be a recall topic, since principle (A) and triage (B) produce identical traces.

**Recall procedure.** The Lyle discipline held and paid: anchoring to seq + observable facts (or the participant's own marker words) produced long, specific reconstructions; no question led, and the method tolerated an unresolved answer (b6) instead of forcing closure — that tolerance should be written into the interview guide ("undecidable" is a valid outcome). Two lessons: (1) participant flags under-sample systematically (flag fatigue, A; single flag, B) — the researcher must pre-select anchors from the trace with a fixed checklist (all rejects, all "Other", post-acceptance events, latency outliers excluding cycle 1, streak gradients, empty sections); this rehearsal did, and items b3/b4/b7/b9 came from researcher-selected anchors, not flags. (2) Both interviews were consistent with the think-aloud ground truth — encouraging for recall fidelity, but weak evidence (synthetic self-consistency); in the real study the think-aloud channel will not exist, which is precisely why the trace mirror + prompt recall must happen soon after the session. Declare the mirror's reactivity (B reflected *because* the counters confronted him) as a designed feature, not a confound to hide.

## (e) GRAITE-vision fit

- **Pedagogical.** The instrument does not merely record practice; the trace mirror occasioned it — B's only reflective moment was induced by "✓ 5 accepted" ("made me notice my own tempo… Mirror-odd"). An instrument that teaches the stance it studies is an asset for a graduate school about critical AI integration — provided the reactivity is declared (d).
- **Meaningful.** Both personas produced material they would actually use; the tool served B's honest goal ("I was looking for done") without forcing performative reflection, and served A's adversarial precision equally. The boilerplate leak is the one violation: tool debris in a seminar plan disrespects the artifact's real-world dignity (B: "like a price sticker left on a gift").
- **Ethical.** Withdraw-and-wipe, local-only storage, and the deliberate snapshot (not keystroke) granularity are the right posture; consent needs the (d) revisions; every proposed telemetry addition (card dwell) must clear data minimisation first. The flag-fatigue observation is also ethical: an instrument that makes participants "argue with a form" externalises research costs onto them.
- **Democratic.** The strongest and least anticipated theme: both participants managed authorship *for an audience* — A disclosing ("Naming who wrote what is not vanity; it is answerability"), B concealing (the projector). Answerability for AI-assisted work surfaced as concrete artifact practice, unprompted, in both profiles. Design principle candidate: make authorship legible **in the artifact itself** (exportable provenance annotations), not only in the researcher's log — turning the study's democratic commitment into a feature participants can use on their own terms.

## (f) RQ1–3 serviceability + refinement PROPOSALS (Phong owns all wording; these are drafts to react to, not positions)

- **RQ1** — *serviceable.* Judgment moments are trace-locatable and recall-constituted; the rehearsal adds that judgment includes preparatory anchoring (b8), withholding/non-use, and self-directed skepticism (b6). PROPOSAL: extend scope to make non-use visible — e.g. "…enact professional judgment and agency when co-creating with a generative AI agent, **including when, where, and why they decline its involvement**."
- **RQ2** — *serviceable at the shape level; typing requires interpretation.* The two profiles separate cleanly on trace shape (prompt length, latency structure, similarity bands, manual-edit volume), but every shape metric inverted meaning at least once across profiles (b1, b3, b5; §a intentionality row). PROPOSAL: make participant interpretation constitutive rather than confirmatory — e.g. "What interaction patterns — sequences of suggestion, acceptance, modification, rejection, and override, **as interpreted with participants in stimulated recall** — characterise…". Consider whether "uncritical acceptance" language should yield to a satisficing–deference spectrum, since B's streak contained three distinct acts.
- **RQ3** — *already yielding.* Candidate principles from two synthetic sessions: near-zero-cost expressive rationale capture; provenance that distinguishes structure from surface; clean suggestion content; representable non-use; tempo made visible (the mirror as intervention); session boundaries; artifact-legible authorship. PROPOSAL: extend the intentionality clause toward answerability — e.g. "…support teacher educators in maintaining pedagogical intentionality **and in making the authorship of AI-assisted materials answerable to those they teach and work with** — and in modelling both for student teachers."

## (g) Feasibility notes for a year-1 doktorand

- **The schema survived contact.** v0.1's event vocabulary located every judgment episode both personas produced; all fixes in (c) are additive (new fields, one new state, UI affordances), not redesigns. Propose as schema v0.2 through the Decision Register per design.md's own rule. Estimated effort: days, not weeks, within the ponytail discipline.
- **Two synthetic sessions reached instrument-bug saturation** (the second session found one new class of bug — latency/orientation — and re-confirmed the rest). A real pilot of 3–5 participants after the (c) fixes is enough for instrument validation before any main study; resist scaling before then.
- **Recall economics.** 6 anchors/participant filled 30–45 interview minutes; anchor pre-selection from a trace takes under an hour with the (d) checklist. Interviews scale linearly with participants and dominate the time budget — plan transcription and analysis time accordingly, and hold the 6-anchor discipline (more anchors dilute reconstruction quality).
- **Analysis tooling.** Everything used here was hand-computation over JSON; simple scripts (latency tables, similarity bands, per-section delegation profiles) suffice for a pilot. Defer process-mining/ENA tooling until the pattern typology stabilises — the rehearsal shows the typology depends on recall, so premature automation would encode exactly the trace-only errors documented in (b).
- **Ethics sequencing.** Consent-text revisions and the who-reads/which-RQ statement belong in the etikprövning package; the provider decision remains GDPR-first (design.md §5). Nothing in this rehearsal touched real data; keep that boundary loud in every derived document.
- **Epistemic hygiene.** This rehearsal validates instrument logic under two deliberately polar personas; it says nothing about actual teacher educators, and its neat A/B contrast will not survive real participants, who will be less internally consistent than personas written to be internally consistent. Treat every pattern here as a hypothesis for the pilot, and keep the SYNTHETIC label attached to anything quoted onward.
